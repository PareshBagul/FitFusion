Animal Movements :-

How to:
1. Start on your hands and knees, with your wrists aligned under your shoulders and knees under your hips.
2. Lift your knees slightly off the ground, keeping a flat back.
3. Move forward by taking small steps with your hands and feet.
4. For added fun, make bear sounds or create a little bear adventure story.


How to:
1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart.
2. Squat down, bringing your hands to the ground.
3. Explosively jump forward, swinging your arms for momentum.
4. Land softly on both feet and immediately go into the next jump.
5. Repeat, enjoying the feeling of hopping like a frog.


How to:
1. Sit on the floor with your hands behind you, fingers pointing away.
2. Lift your hips off the ground, supporting your weight with your hands and feet.
3. Crawl backward, sideways, or in a circle using your hands and feet.
4. Engage your core muscles for stability.

Encourage creativity, imagination, and most importantly, make it a fun experience for the kids. These movements not only promote physical activity but also enhance coordination and motor skills.

How to:
1. Lie on your stomach with your arms extended in front of you.
2. Lift your upper body slightly off the ground.
3. Wiggle forward by moving your upper body and hips in a snake-like motion.
4. Engage your core and enjoy the stretch along your spine.

3. Swing your arms for balance and momentum.
4. Repeat the hopping motion, imagining you're a kangaroo bounding through the outback.
